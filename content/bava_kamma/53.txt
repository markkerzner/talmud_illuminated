Bava Kamma 53 - A Man and An Ox Together Push another Ox into a Pit  (Torts)
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_hGn1v4stHxE/SZ4SxEmUJ2I/AAAAAAAAA_I/wZCX-TQrStM/s1600-h/John+Quidor+-+The+Gold+Diggers+1832.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5304698045346359138" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/_hGn1v4stHxE/SZ4SxEmUJ2I/AAAAAAAAA_I/wZCX-TQrStM/s320/John+Quidor+-+The+Gold+Diggers+1832.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; float: left; height: 242px; margin: 0pt 10px 10px 0pt; width: 320px;" /></a>If a worker digs a pit, he, and not his employer, is liable for the damages - because "there is no messenger for transgression," and it is forbidden to  create damaging obstacles  in the public domain.<br />
<br />
If someone pushes an ox into a pit or startles the animal and it falls, the owner of the pit is liable for whatever can't be collected from the first man.<br />
<br />
If an ox and a person together push either a person or an animal or utensils into a pit, all (the person, the ox, and the pit) are liable for damages. Still, only a human is liable for the "<a href="https://talmudilluminated.com/bava_kamma/bava_kamma26.html" target="_blank">four payments</a>."<br />
