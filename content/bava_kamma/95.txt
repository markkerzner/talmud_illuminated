Bava Kamma 95 - One Stole a Pregnant Cow, and it Gave Birth
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_hGn1v4stHxE/SdUzHPueBZI/AAAAAAAABHE/iYvoA28fHF4/s1600-h/Alexander+Mann+-+Sheep+Shearing.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5320214734381647250" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/_hGn1v4stHxE/SdUzHPueBZI/AAAAAAAABHE/iYvoA28fHF4/s320/Alexander+Mann+-+Sheep+Shearing.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; float: left; height: 320px; margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px; width: 266px;" /></a><div>If one stole a pregnant cow, and it gave birth, or a wool-laden ewe, and he sheared it, he pays the value of a cow ready to give birth, or the value of a ewe prepared to be shorn.</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Other opinions:</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
If one steals an ewe and he sheared it, or a cow and it gave birth...</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
* Rabbi Meir – He pays it, its shearings, and its offspring;</div>
<div>
* Rabbi Yehuda – the stolen animal returns to the original owner as is;</div>
<div>
* Rabbi Shimon – We view it as an article appraised and placed with him according to its cash value at the time of the robbery.</div><div><br /></div><div>Art: Sheep Shearing by Alexander Mann</div>
