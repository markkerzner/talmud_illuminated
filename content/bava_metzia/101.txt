<div>
<span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: medium;">If one rents a house to his fellow, he cannot evict the tenant in the winter, i.e. from Succoth until Passover - unless he gives the tenant notice in the summer, at least thirty days before the beginning of Succoth, because during winter rental houses in Israel were hard to find.</span></div>
<div>
<span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: medium;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: medium;">In large cities, where there is a perennial housing shortage, a landlord must give twelve months’ notice before evicting a tenant. </span></div>
<div>
<span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: medium;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: medium;">With regard to stores, both in the towns and in the large cities a landlord must give twelve months’ notice, to allow the storeowners to start collecting on the credit they usually extend to their customers. </span></div>
