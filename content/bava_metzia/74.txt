Bava Metzia 74 - Commodity Loans
<span style="font-size: 100%;"><a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_hGn1v4stHxE/SlUDhzcWV8I/AAAAAAAACIQ/AfZP6ZoJMu4/s1600-h/Vincent+Van+Gogh+-+Still+Life+With+Two+Sacks+And+A+Bottle.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5356191211106752450" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/_hGn1v4stHxE/SlUDhzcWV8I/AAAAAAAACIQ/AfZP6ZoJMu4/s320/Vincent+Van+Gogh+-+Still+Life+With+Two+Sacks+And+A+Bottle.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; float: left; height: 237px; margin: 0pt 10px 10px 0pt; width: 320px;" /></a></span><span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: 130%;">Commodity loans - such as a loan of a bag of wheat to be repaid with a bag of wheat later - are generally prohibited. If the price of the commodity increases in the interim, the lender will receive back more value than he lent, which constitutes interest. The correct way to give such loans is to estimate the value of the bag when it is given and later return the amount of wheat of equal value.</span><br />
