Ketubot 7 – Blessing at the wedding
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ZxCu3InX-Mg/VOOD1IyGt0I/AAAAAAAAXXI/97aw8oPRpjM/s1600/The%2BEngagement%2BRing%2Bby%2BJohn%2BShirley%2BFox.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ZxCu3InX-Mg/VOOD1IyGt0I/AAAAAAAAXXI/97aw8oPRpjM/s1600/The%2BEngagement%2BRing%2Bby%2BJohn%2BShirley%2BFox.jpg" height="320" width="238" /></a>A marriage is a mitzvah which requires the following blessing to be said: Blessed are You, God our Lord, King of the universe, who sanctified us with His commandments and commanded us regarding not violating forbidden unions; Who forbade us to even stay alone with our bride before we are married, and Who permitted us those women who are married to us – but through proper chuppah, which is preceded by engagement. 
<br />
<br />
And then it concludes, just like a Kiddush – Blessed are You, God, Who sanctified Israel with chuppah and engagement.
